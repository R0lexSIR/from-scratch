Software Modelling and Design

Course Relevance:
Software Engineering, rooted in both Computer Science and Computer Engineering, offers two perspectives: Computer Science-first or Software Engineering-first. It encompasses the entire software lifecycle, aiming to create high-quality, reliable programs systematically. This involves using formal methods for specification, analysis, design, and evaluation. It demands scalable software development techniques, meeting timing, size, and security criteria within project budgets and deadlines. The field draws on analytical and descriptive tools from Computer Science and the rigor of Computer Engineering, ensuring trustworthiness in the systems developed by collaborative teams.

Web Technology

Course Relevance:
In the information age, global communication is paramount. Web technology, a global field, enables communication through websites and underpins IT infrastructures worldwide. It facilitates the rapid dissemination of technologies and applications. This course's main goal is to teach fundamental web technology concepts necessary for web application development. It covers descriptive languages, server-side and client-side programming, and includes content on relational database communication and information security. Engineering Design and Innovation

Course Relevance:
Project Centric Learning (PCL) empowers students by allowing them to explore their interests and strengths. In addition to regular coursework, it introduces semester-long Engineering Design and Development courses to address real-world issues within domains like healthcare, agriculture, defense, and more. Students supplement their knowledge with online courses and faculty guidance, preparing them for a project-driven world. PCL fosters active, inquiry-based learning, transforming teachers into mentors. It engages students in tackling complex, open-ended questions and challenges, promoting higher-order thinking skills, moving beyond knowledge acquisition to analysis, design, and application, aligning with Bloom's Taxonomy categories. PCL equips students for success in a project-centric society.
